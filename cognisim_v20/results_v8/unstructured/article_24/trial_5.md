Title: DORA Compliance Test Plan for Article 24 - Large Bank

1. Objective:
The objective of this test plan is to ensure that the large bank complies with the Data Ownership, Responsibility, and Accountability (DORA) framework as outlined in Article 24 of relevant regulations. The testing will focus on evaluating the bank's data ownership, responsibility, and accountability practices to ensure data protection and privacy.

2. Scope:
This test plan will cover the following key areas of Article 24 compliance for the large bank:
- Data ownership: Examining how data ownership is defined, assigned, and managed within the organization.
- Data responsibility: Assessing the procedures in place for ensuring data protection, including access controls and data security measures.
- Data accountability: Evaluating the mechanisms for monitoring and enforcing data protection policies and procedures.

3. Test Approach:
The compliance testing will be conducted through a combination of document review, interviews with key stakeholders, and testing of systems and processes. The testing will focus on verifying adherence to the principles outlined in Article 24 and identifying any gaps or areas for improvement.

4. Test Activities:
4.1. Document Review:
- Review the bank's data ownership policies, procedures, and guidelines.
- Examine data responsibility frameworks, including access control mechanisms and data security protocols.
- Evaluate data accountability mechanisms, such as monitoring and reporting procedures.

4.2. Stakeholder Interviews:
- Interview key personnel responsible for data management, security, and compliance.
- Discuss data ownership, responsibility, and accountability practices within the organization.
- Seek clarification on roles and responsibilities related to data protection and privacy.

4.3. System and Process Testing:
- Assess the effectiveness of access controls and data security measures in place.
- Test data protection mechanisms to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.
- Validate data monitoring and reporting processes to ensure accountability.

5. Deliverables:
- Compliance test report outlining findings, observations, and recommendations.
- Action plan detailing remediation steps for addressing any identified gaps.
- Stakeholder presentation to communicate test results and recommendations for improvement.
