Creating a compliance test plan for Article 25 of the Data Protection Regulation for a large bank involves ensuring that the bank is compliant with the principles of data protection by design and by default. Here is a sample test plan outline:

1. **Objective**: The objective of this compliance test plan is to assess the bank's adherence to the requirements of Article 25 of the Data Protection Regulation, focusing on data protection by design and by default.

2. **Scope**: The scope of this test plan covers all systems, processes, and applications within the bank that involve the processing of personal data.

3. **Test Criteria**:
   - Review of documented data protection policies and procedures
   - Assessment of data protection measures implemented by the bank
   - Evaluation of data protection impact assessments (DPIAs)
   - Examination of data minimization and purpose limitation practices
   - Analysis of data security measures in place

4. **Test Activities**:
   a. Review of Data Protection Policies and Procedures:
      - Assess the adequacy and effectiveness of the bank's data protection policies and procedures
      - Verify that the policies align with the requirements of Article 25

   b. Data Protection Measures Evaluation:
      - Evaluate the technical and organizational measures implemented by the bank for data protection
      - Check if encryption, pseudonymization, and access controls are in place

   c. Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs):
      - Review DPIAs conducted by the bank for relevant projects and systems
      - Ensure that DPIAs are carried out in accordance with regulatory requirements

   d. Data Minimization and Purpose Limitation:
      - Verify that the bank collects only necessary data for specified purposes
      - Check if data retention periods are defined and enforced

   e. Data Security Measures:
      - Assess the bank's data security practices, including incident response and breach notification procedures
      - Review access controls, data encryption, and other security measures

5. **Reporting**:
   - Prepare a detailed report outlining the findings from the compliance test plan
   - Provide recommendations for areas needing improvement or remediation

6. **Timeline**:
   - Define the timeline for conducting the compliance test plan activities, including testing, analysis, and reporting

7. **Responsibilities**:
   - Assign roles and responsibilities for executing the compliance test plan activities
   - Ensure coordination between relevant departments and stakeholders

8. **Review and Approval**:
   - Obtain necessary approvals for the compliance test plan before initiation
   - Conduct a review of the test plan results with key stakeholders

By following this compliance test plan outline, the large bank can assess its compliance with data protection requirements under Article 25 of the Data Protection Regulation and take necessary actions to enhance its data protection practices.
